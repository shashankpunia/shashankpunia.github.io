DriX

  • by:Shashank Punia
  • date:Nov 8, 2016
  • 2 Awards

Project DriX is an advanced driving behavior recognition system, comprising of a set of sensors embedded in the driver’s console which discretize driving patterns on a highly individualized level. These parameters are used to develop a sophisticated database of multiple drivers using the car, which is later implemented for enhancing user’s driving experience and preventing accidents based on pattern discrepancies or predictions.

These sensors are embedded on the dashboard, steering wheel, driver seat and car control pedals (clutch, break and throttle) to gather tactile, biometric and medical MEMs(body temperature, heart rate, skin sweat, facial micro-expressions) based data which is used to recognize real-time status of the driver; in terms of natural or unnatural driving behavior, health and wellness, medical condition(s), mental stress, HF&E - human factors and ergonomics. The raw data from the sensors are used to discretize a user model/profile for further implementations to improve driving experience through feedbacks (haptic and voice-enabled), ensure safety and prevention of accidents by data extrapolation. Moreover, the data from multiple system nodes being driven can be used for an intricate database of driving pattern for big data analysis.

The Problem

1. A large number of car accidents occur due to medical condition(s) which can be prevented by time syntactic intelligent measurement of health parameters by an in-car system, where a machine level intervention may be required.
2. An abundance of scrupulous driving behavior data is called for, in regards to improve self-driven algorithms.
3. A driver model or profiling is crucial for car companies, to maintain a record which keeps track of driving proficiency of the user, driver’s health and wellness, an individualized set of driver-specific information – circumstantial driving pattern/behavior, data necessary for safety and instances purposes.

How does DriX solve the problem?

An exhaustive biometric and medical parametrical analysis of the driver which can be further implemented for driving safety and instantaneous driving behavior. Facial recognition (FACS -Facial Action Coding System based) based analysis of sleep patterns – fatigue, stress level and anxiety. Further, An infotainment system which is programmed to make mechanical level interventions- to park the vehicle safely and automatically call for emergency while also information friends and family of the user.
Feedbacks based on monitoring of stress levels via FACS as well as health/wellness monitoring sensors. The voice enabled personal assistant can help reduce the stress level by using personalized user data and modifying the music playlist accordingly. Haptic feedback via pedals and driver seat for a varied range of notifications, for eg – speed limit, drowsiness detection and alarm etc.

Applications

- Customizability and ease of driving for a multi-user vehicle.
- Driving methodology/behavior pattern database for autonomous/self-driven vehicle algorithms (data mining).
- Stress reduction based on comprehensive driving anxiety detection- intelligent control of music, air conditioning, seat position/ergonomics and personalized responses from in-car personal assistant. Also prevent driver fatigue or panic - for a comfortable and relieved driving experience.
- Seat anthropometry tuning/Driving posture in accordance to driving situation.
- Haptic sensors on seat and steering for alerts – speed limit, medical conditions, and traffic etiquette and regulation
- Intelligent driving personal assistant for an interactive environment, user feedback, troubleshooting in case of car breakdown.
- Improving driver’s proficiency in vehicle handling by monitoring time syntactic driving pattern and overall engine performance.
